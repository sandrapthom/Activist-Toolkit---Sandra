Discoveries made by Women

Edith Clarke

Clarke was an electrical engineer in the 20th century. Although she struggled to find jobs in this path, she became the first professionally employed female electrical engineer in the United States in 1922.

Mollie Orshansky

She was a food economist and statistician who used her skills to create a standard on how new policies affect people in poverty. Her impact lasts to this day with these standards still in use.

Grace Hopper

Hopper worked on a great deal of computer and programming language development from the 1930s through the 1980s. She has developed many computer languages. One of these include COBOL which is still used today.

By 2019, woman only made up 27% of the STEM career field in the US.
